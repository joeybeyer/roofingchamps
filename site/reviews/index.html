Why this page might look different

Real Feedback, Not Invented Quotes

The honest version: most roofing sites greet you with "4.9 stars from 437 reviews" and a wall of testimonials. Click any of them. Try to trace them back. Usually you can't. We chose not to play that game.

So this page might look light compared to those. That's the point. When a real homeowner sends real feedback after we've helped with their roof, it goes here - exactly as they wrote it. Empty space beats invented quotes, every time.

How we handle this

Our Review Policy In Plain English

Verified people only

Reviews only show up here when an actual homeowner who used Roofing Champs sends them in. No purchased reviews, no AI-generated ones, no "borrowed" testimonials from a sister site.

No magic star badges

You won't see a "4.9 stars from 437 reviews" graphic that can't be verified. If we ever post review data, it'll trace to specific feedback.

Negative reviews stay

If a homeowner had a bad experience and shares it, that stays up too. Hiding negative feedback is the same as making up positive feedback - both lie about reality.

Published as written

Aside from minor formatting cleanup, comments appear exactly the way you sent them. We're not in the business of polishing your review to sound more enthusiastic.

After the work's done

How To Leave A Review

  1. Wait until the work's actually complete and you've had a couple weeks to see how it holds up.
  2. Tell us how it went - the request process, the roofing pro, the work itself, the outcome. The good and the bad.
  3. If you want it published here, let us know and we'll add it with your permission.
  4. If you'd rather post on a third-party review platform, ask for the right link and we'll send it.

Answers for homeowners

Frequently Asked Questions

Do you publish fake reviews?

No. We'd rather show you a mostly empty page than make up testimonials. Real feedback from real homeowners only - and yeah, that means this page might look sparse compared to sites with 400 fabricated 5-star quotes.

Can I leave a review after my roof work?

Please do. Once your request has been handled and the work's complete, share what your experience was actually like. Future homeowners read this stuff.

Where else can I leave a review?

Third-party review sites are honestly more useful for future homeowners - they're verified differently. Reach out after the work's done and we'll point you to the right place.

What if my experience was bad?

Tell us. Seriously. Honest negative feedback is more valuable than no feedback - it tells us where the routing process broke and gives future homeowners a real picture, not a marketing one.

Are reviews edited?

Just basic formatting (typos, line breaks). The content stays as the homeowner wrote it. We don't edit reviews to sound more positive than they are.
